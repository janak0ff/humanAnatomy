circulatory system



The circulatory system consists of the heart, blood vessels, and the blood. Its main functions are:

  1. Transport oxygen and nutrients - The circulatory system transports oxygen and nutrients from the lungs and digestive system to all the cells and tissues of the body. Red blood cells in the blood carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body.

  2. Remove waste - The circulatory system removes waste products from cells and tissues. It transports carbon dioxide, a waste product, from the cells to the lungs to be exhaled. It also carries other waste products to the kidneys and liver for disposal.

  3. Fight disease - The circulatory system helps fight disease and infection in the body. White blood cells, or leukocytes, patrol the body and attack foreign substances like bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens.

  4. Regulate temperature - The circulatory system helps maintain homeostasis in the body, including temperature regulation. When the body gets too hot, the circulatory system diverts more blood to the skin so that heat can escape. When the body gets too cold, less blood flows to the skin.

  5. Transport hormones - The circulatory system transports hormones from endocrine glands to the tissues and organs that need them. For example, thyroid hormones are carried from the thyroid gland to organs like the heart and muscles.

The main components of the circulatory system are:

  1. Heart - The heart acts as a pump to circulate blood throughout the body. It receives deoxygenated blood from the body and pumps it to the lungs. It also receives oxygenated blood from the lungs and pumps it out to the body.

  2. Blood vessels - A network of arteries, veins, and capillaries transport blood to and from the heart and deliver it to all parts of the body.

  3. Blood - The blood is the transport medium and contains red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma.

The vital circulatory system responsible for blood circulation consists of a muscular heart and a system of blood vessels and is also known as the cardiovascular system. It is a critical link between the neuromuscular system and the organs.
